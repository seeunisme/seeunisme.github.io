About this thesis

I’m exploring how multi-sensory entertainment can feel rich, expressive, and joyful for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing people — not as an afterthought, but as the starting point.

Big question

Most entertainment is built around sound first, and then captions are added on top. I’m asking:

“What if we designed for vibration, light, motion, and touch from the very beginning, not just as ‘accessibility features’?”

What you’ll see here

  • Small experiments and sketches instead of polished products.
  • Notes from sessions and playtests (with details anonymized).
  • Questions I’m stuck on where your perspective can really help.

How your feedback helps

Your reactions — even a single emoji — help me see what feels exciting, confusing, or off. I use this feedback to:

  • Choose which ideas to keep exploring.
  • Notice what feels missing or hard to read.
  • Make the final thesis less academic and more human.

Who this is for

  • Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing folks (primary focus).
  • Friends, interpreters, and collaborators.
  • Designers and accessibility advocates curious about this space.

This site is a small, low-pressure space by design. You do not need to explain your whole story or write paragraphs. Thank you for any time and energy you share here.
